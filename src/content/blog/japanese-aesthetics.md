## What is "Ma"?

"Ma" is a Japanese concept that refers not only to physical space but also to temporal and spiritual emptiness.

In architecture, music, tea ceremony, and design, "Ma" plays a vital role.

## Applying Ma to Web Design

### Whitespace

Ample whitespace provides a visual "breathing room."

- Line height: `line-height: 1.8` — Natural readability for text
- Letter spacing: `letter-spacing: 0.1em` — Allowing each character to breathe
- Paragraph margins: `24px` or more

### Typographic Restraint

Fewer colors, limited font weights. Elegance emerges through restraint.

```css
--font-primary: 'Zen Kaku Gothic New', sans-serif;
--text-body: 0.9375rem;
--lh-body: 1.8;
--ls-base: 0.1em;
```

### Color Palette Constraints

Tsubaki uses only five colors:

| Purpose | Color |
|---------|-------|
| Background | `#F3F0EA` — Warm cream |
| Text | `#2A2A2A` — Near black |
| Subtext | `#727272` — Grey |
| Accent | `#C9BC9C` — Warm gold |
| Border | `#DDD8CE` — Pale cream |

## Conclusion

"Ma" in design is not about what you add, but about what you remove.

> Less is more — and sometimes, the most powerful statement is the space between words.
